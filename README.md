Key Insights

Drug-related searches dominate activity
Controlled drugs account for 57.9% of all searches, significantly higher than any other category

Low arrest conversion rate
Only 17.6% of searches lead to arrest, meaning the majority of searches do not result in outcomes

Younger age groups are most targeted
Individuals aged 18–34 represent the highest proportion of searches, indicating a strong focus on this demographic

Search activity is consistent with seasonal spikes
Monthly searches remain stable overall, with a noticeable increase in August, suggesting seasonal or operational factors

Ethnicity distribution shows concentration in key groups
The majority of searches involve Black and White individuals, highlighting the need for further contextual analysis

Business Recommendations

Improve targeting efficiency
Refine intelligence-led searches to increase arrest conversion rates and reduce unnecessary stops

Focus on high-impact categories
Prioritise drug-related operations where activity is highest, while reviewing low-yield categories

Review demographic targeting
Assess whether current focus on younger age groups aligns with actual arrest outcomes

Align resources with demand patterns
Investigate seasonal spikes (e.g. August) and allocate resources accordingly
